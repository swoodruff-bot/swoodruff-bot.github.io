Agile Project Design  
Seamus Woodruff  
Agile Design Process  
This project followed an iterative, agile design process in which my redesign evolved through ongoing  
analysis, research, and discussion rather than being fixed from the outset. Design decisions were  
continuously reassessed in response to academic research, user discourse drawn from Reddit, my own  
5A's analysis of Apple Screen Time.  
Considered but Unpursued Directions  
Several design ideas were explored but not included in the final proposal. My original redesign concept  
centered far more around the individual user, specifically making limits far harder to overcome. More  
aggressive enforcement mechanisms, such as hard lockouts were considered but ultimately rejected in  
favor of balancing effective limits with user autonomy. I had also originally planned on focusing my  
redesign away from parental controls in favor of prioritizing individual users, but ultimately my research  
steered me back towards parental controls being an essential component. Along similar lines I had  
planned on making Screen Time a stand alone app in order to improve UI and setup, but ultimately  
decided to pivot towards including it in the health app due to what my academic research was revealing,  
and at the suggestion of Julie Felner during an informational interview, while asking for her personal  
opinion on my proposed redesigns.  
Iteration Toward the Final Design  
Early redesign concepts focused primarily on fixing issues I had personally experienced with Screen  
Times core functionality. But, analysis of user feedback revealed that frustration centered not only on  
Screen Times core functionality but also its UI, setup, and a lack of features. These insights, alongside  
my research into the effects of screen time, shifted the redesign toward not just fixing existing issues with  
functionality, but also improving clarity, usability, and features that would help users have a healthier  
relationship with their devices. The decision to integrate Screen Time into the Health app emerged  
iteratively, as it addressed multiple issues at once: UI confusion, lack of longitudinal context, absence of  
guidance, and a recontextualization of the tool around health and wellbeing. The Health app was also  
preferable over a stand alone app as it is already widely used, offers an intuitive UI, and doesnt introduce  
an additional app users need to have on their phone. Parental features similarly evolved due to a need for  
health related screen time interventions for adolescents, that I became increasingly aware of through my  
own research as well as Professor Nascimentos Class Smart Phones, Mobile Selves.  
Data Limitations and Ideal Data  
This project relied on existing academic literature, Reddit-based qualitative analysis, and my own  
structural analysis of Apples Screen Time system. While sufficient for identifying shortcomings of  
Screen Time and guiding redesigns, these data sources have limitations. Reddit users are not  
representative of all populations and inherently represent a level of self selection bias on the topics they  
are discussing. Academic studies, while hugely useful for justifying the necessity of this redesign, seldom  
address Screen Time directly and are primarily focused on the effect of excessive screen use on  
adolescents.  
My Ideal data would include a survey of a large and representative sample of adult Iphone users. It would  
collect data on their relation with screen use, any negative or positive outcomes they have observed in  
relation to their phone use, a section about their health and wellbeing, their Screen Time data, any issues  
theyve had with Apple Screen Time, and any features they wish were a part of Screen Time. This dataset  
would allow for much more in depth analysis, and let me run my own regressions to look at how screen  
use affects adult users negatively or positively.  
Next Steps  
If I had had more time or resources, or should someone else wish to continue pursuing this issue in the  
future, I believe the most meaningful steps are those that would encourage Apple itself to implement these  
recommendations. While there is certainly an argument for an individual creating an app that meets many  
of my suggestions, I do not believe that is necessarily the right path. There are already many user created  
apps that implement much better approaches to screen time limiting than Apple Screen Time. But, there is  
a reason that many users continue to use Screen Time instead of these apps; it is convenient and doesnt  
require searching out and installing an entirely new app. On top of that, Apple has the ability to  
implement changes and add features that simply arent available to third party developers, and is uniquely  
positioned to bring more awareness about screen use related health issues to its customers.  
I believe that getting more concrete evidence that an overhaul of Screen Time is something that  
consumers are truly interested in and presenting that evidence to Apple would be the most productive next  
step for this project.
